work

rapStudy (2019 - now)

I'm head of product at rapStudy. We're an Edtech company that integrates educational content into kids' favorite songs. Check us out here.

I joined the team in late 2019, and we've gone from an idea and a demo .mp3 to a full web app with users from over 30 schools in NY, CT, and NJ, built with React.js and Firebase.

The key problem we're trying to solve is student engagement. Studies have repeatedly shown that lower engagement results in lower achievement (and this is especially true at younger ages).

By using music that kids already know and love, we provide a way for kids to get excited about learning by singing and dancing - whether its over Zoom or back at school.


Bowtie (2019)

In the summer of 2019, I interned at Bowtie, an Insurtech startup based in Hong Kong. There, I worked on identifying new customer segments, and wrote tailored Python tools with NLTK to identify hard-to-read areas in their insurance policies.

The core issue was readability - as a company that markets policies directly to the end user, customers only understand Bowtie's unique value if they can understand their policies.

With these tools, I was able to identify the specific topics and segments in the policies that were too dense, long, or burdened with legalese.
